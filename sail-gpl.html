Why SAIL‑GPL

Open weights that no one can verify are not open. They are forgeable.

An AI model is a claim about how it was made. Yet most model licenses say nothing about whether that claim can be checked. The supply chain became the attack surface — and the license layer never caught up.

01 · TAMPER

Poisoned & swapped

Poisoned packages have compromised tens of thousands of AI pipelines. Malicious LoRA adapters and model‑source tampering ride into deployments unnoticed.

02 · NO PROVENANCE

Weights without origin

Model cards offer no guarantee of origin. Once a model is fine‑tuned, merged and re‑hosted a few times, its lineage — and its trust — collapses.

03 · VOLUNTARY ONLY

Good tools, no obligation

OMS signing, Sigstore transparency, CoSAI and SAIF are excellent — but optional. Nothing makes secure release the default at the licensing layer.

THE GAP

Ethics ≠ security

OpenRAIL propagates ethical‑use restrictions downstream. OpenMDW & Apache‑2.0 grant permissive freedom. Neither makes integrity, provenance and security a copyleft obligation.

THE FIX

Security as copyleft

SAIL‑GPL takes RAIL's proven propagation mechanism and points it at cybersecurity: what rides the model, rides every derivative. Strip the security, lose the license.

Principles

Five principles of SAIL‑GPL

01

Verifiable Origin

A model that cannot be cryptographically traced to its maker is untrusted by default. Unsigned is unsafe.

02

Inseparable Security

Security obligations are entangled with the weights. Fine‑tuning, quantizing, merging or re‑hosting cannot remove them — only renew them.

03

Independence from Infrastructure

You may own the GPUs, the cluster, the serving stack. You do not own an exemption. The duty attaches to the artifact, wherever it runs.

04

Transparency of Risk

Capability without disclosed limits is a liability. Known weaknesses, evaluations and intended‑use boundaries travel with the model.

05

Coordinated Resilience

Integrity must outlive one release: revisions are re‑signed, algorithms stay agile and post‑quantum‑ready, and vulnerabilities are disclosed, not buried.

Section 7 · the core

Secure‑Release Metadata

Every distribution must ship a complete, verifiable SRM bundle. A release without it is a license violation (curable within 30 days). Obligations scale with capability tier.

§7.1

Signed provenance

An OpenSSF Model Signing (OMS) signature over a manifest hashing all files — weights, config, tokenizer, code. Keyless (Sigstore), PKI, or hybrid.

§7.2

AI Bill of Materials

Machine‑readable AIBOM: base model, datasets or data‑provenance statement, adapters, dependencies, pipeline. SPDX 3.0 AI / CycloneDX‑ML.

§7.3

Model card and risk

Intended use, out‑of‑scope use, known failure modes, security‑relevant evals — aligned to the NIST AI RMF.

§7.4

Adversarial attestation

Signed test results vs. OWASP LLM Top 10, MITRE ATLAS and the CoSAI Risk Map: prompt injection, data extraction, poisoning.

§7.5

Crypto‑agility

Declared algorithms, re‑signing on revision, and post‑quantum / hybrid signatures (ML‑DSA, FIPS 204) for long‑lived & frontier models.

§7.6

Coordinated disclosure

A published security contact and CVD policy (SECURITY.md) that propagates to every derivative.

§7.7

Open verification

The format and tooling to verify compliance must be open and re‑implementable — no single‑vendor, fee‑bearing checks.

§7.8

Re‑attest on derivation

Fine‑tune, merge or quantize, then re‑sign, update the AIBOM, refresh the card, and attest new risks. Security is renewed, never discharged.

Obligations scale with risk

T0
Research / not conveyed

Internal only — nothing triggered. Distribution moves you to at least T1.

T1
Open release, low capability

Signing, AIBOM, model card, CVD, open verification. Lightweight self‑attestation.

T2
General‑purpose / product‑embedded

All of §7, with the full minimum adversarial test set. Hybrid post‑quantum signing recommended.

T3
Frontier / high‑risk (EU AI Act Annex III‑type)

Strongest setting, with an independent red‑team. PQ/hybrid signatures and transparency‑log inclusion required.

2 · The bundle

model/
├─ model.safetensors
├─ config.json
├─ tokenizer.json
├─ MODEL_CARD.md
└─ srm/
   ├─ manifest.json            # hashes of all model files (OMS)
   ├─ model.sig                # detached signature (ML-DSA / ECDSA / hybrid)
   ├─ aibom.spdx.json          # AI Bill of Materials (SPDX 3.0 AI / CycloneDX-ML)
   ├─ security-attestation.json# §7.4 adversarial test results, signed
   └─ SECURITY.md              # §7.6 CVD policy & contact

3 · The toolkit — one CLI, sail

sail sign ./model/      # build the OMS manifest, sign it (keyless Sigstore / PKI / PQ-hybrid), write srm/
sail verify ./model/    # check signature, manifest completeness, AIBOM, attestation, CVD contact  [--pq-only --strict-issuer]
sail audit ./release/   # walk a directory tree, report artifacts missing compliant §7 SRM
